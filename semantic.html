Introduction

Warangal is a historic city located in the southern Indian state of Telangana. It was once the capital of the Kakatiya dynasty, which ruled the region from the 12th to the 14th centuries. The city is renowned for its architectural heritage, particularly its forts, temples, and intricate sculptures. Key landmarks include the Warangal Fort, an impressive structure with massive walls and gateways, and the Thousand Pillar Temple, famous for its richly carved pillars and ancient design Warangal's history is marked by its strategic importance and cultural richness. The city was a significant center of trade and culture during the Kakatiya period and has continued to be a major historical and cultural site in modern times.

About Place

Warangal is a city in the Indian state of Telangana. It is the district headquarters of Warangal district and is located about 145 km north of the state capital, Hyderabad. The city is situated on the banks of the Krishna River and is known for its rich history, cultural heritage, and natural beauty.
